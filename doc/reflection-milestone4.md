# Reflection for Milestone 4
For Milestone 4, our main goal was to turn the earlier prototype into a stable and usable dashboard that works well in a full-screen grading environment and is practical for our target users, such as regional dealership managers. Instead of adding many new features, we mainly focused on improving usability, layout clarity, and chart readability based on the feedback we received during previous milestones and the user survey.
One of the most important improvements was restructuring the layout of the dashboard. All filtering controls were moved into a dedicated left-side panel, while the analytical content was placed on the right side with KPI cards and a 2×2 grid of charts. This design makes the dashboard easier to scan and reduces unnecessary scrolling. We also improved label visibility by adding dynamic labels for the selected price and horsepower ranges so that users can immediately understand the current filtering conditions.
Several chart designs were also updated based on TA and peer feedback. For example, the fuel trend chart now has improved spacing for the year axis, making the timeline easier to read. The top models chart uses distinct colors for each model to improve visual differentiation. The top colors chart was simplified to a single consistent color for readability. In addition, the horsepower–price panel was redesigned from a dense scatter-style visualization into LOESS regression trend lines grouped by fuel type. This change makes the overall relationship between horsepower and price easier to interpret during analysis.
Many of these changes were directly motivated by the feedback from Milestone 2. For example, the previous version had issues with label visibility, axis spacing, and full-screen layout behavior. In this milestone we addressed those issues by refining the layout structure, improving chart titles and axis labels, and ensuring that charts behave properly even when filters return very small subsets of data.
During Milestone 3, we also conducted a user survey and received nine responses. The calculated SUS score was 71.4, which indicates that the dashboard is generally usable but still has room for improvement. Users particularly liked the fuel trend and model ranking charts because they clearly show market patterns. However, some users mentioned that filters could be clearer and that the scatter plot became difficult to read when many points overlapped. These comments also influenced the redesign of the horsepower–price visualization.
For stability and performance, we implemented several safeguards. For example, the regression chart samples rows before LOESS fitting to avoid rendering limits in Altair, and fallback messages are shown when the filtered dataset is too small to produce meaningful charts. While we originally considered implementing full chart-to-chart click cross-filtering, we decided to prioritize stability and clear global filtering controls in the final version.
Overall, the feedback from both the teaching team and survey participants helped us improve the dashboard’s usability and deliver a more stable and readable final application.
